How to play Mancala

The board contains 2 rows of 6 holes with 1 large hole i.e. mancala on each end.

To start, 4 beans are placed in each hole, not including the mancalas.

In this rendering of the game, you will start off the game.

You will choose one of the holes on your side. All the beans inside the hole you selected will be dropped one at a time in the succeeding holes going counterclockwise, all the while skipping over your opponent's mancala.

Now 3 events can occur when the distribution of the beans ends:
  1. The last bean lands on an empty hole that's on your side.
  2. The last bean lands on your own mancala.
  3. None of the above

If event 1 occurs, then you get to steal your opponent's beans that are directly across from the hole where the bean landed. The stolen beans and the bean that landed on the empty hole will be placed inside your mancala. Then it's your opponent's turn to play.

If event 2 occurs, it's your turn again to pick a hole. Any and every time it lands on your mancala, you get to go again.

If none of the above occurs, then it's your opponent's turn.

Your opponents will follow the same rules with their side of the board.

The game will end when all 6 of the holes on either side of the board are empty. When this happens, the player that still has some beans left inside the holes on their side, is able to collect those beans and place them in their own mancala.

The beans on each mancala will be tallied up and the mancala with the most beans wins the game.
